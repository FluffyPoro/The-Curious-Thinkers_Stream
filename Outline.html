Stream Outline

  • How long should you stream?

    At least 1 hour or 1 hour and 30 minutes.

  • What do you want to happen in your stream during the duration you set?

    Ask/Talk to interviewees and gain information from their experiences.

  • What is your content?

    Interviewing 3 guests to share their experiences on online and onsite learning and how it affected their productivity.

  • How do you want your content to run in your stream?

    We hope to gain insight on students' opinions regarding online and onsite classes, which is more effective and which mode of learning some of them prefer.

  • Will you hold discussions?

    Yes we will be holding a discussion on the features of online and onsite classes and some pros and cons of each.

  • Will you talk to your viewers while you stream? What are the topics you will talk about?

    We will talk about why do they prefer that mode of learning and how well it works on them.

  • Will you change your content in the middle of the stream? If yes, what are you planning to replace it with?

    We plan on sticking with interviews for the duration of our stream.

  • What are the ways you will interact with the viewers?

    We can interact with the viewers by responding to them in the chat box and answer their questions.

  • Will you be using your audio for the whole duration?

    We will be using microphones for the interview and background music for our intro and outro.

  • What will appear on your screen for the whole duration of your stream?

    Live footage of us interviewing our chosen three grade 10 students.

  • Will you change your overlays every hour?

    We will only use the set of overlays that have been made.

  • Will you have a break? How long will that take?

    There will be no breaks in between.

  • Is there a video you want to play while you are streaming? What are those? Do you own the video? If not, does it follow the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) guidelines?
